This page describes The Arrival of Akrura to Gokula which is chapter 38 of the English translation of the Bhagavata Purana, one of the eighteen major puranas containing roughly 18,000 metrical verses. Topics include ancient Indian history, religion, philosophy, geography, mythology, etc. The text has been interpreted by various schools of philosophy. This is the thirty-eighth chapter of the Tenth Skandha of the Bhagavatapurana.

11. Though he is merely the witness of the Cause and the effect, he is devoid of egoism. By virtue of his lustre (lustre-like Spiritual Knowledge), he has dispelled the darkness of ignorance or Nescience and the consequent notions of differentiation and misapprehensions resulting from it. By his will, he is, however, found to be sportively engaged in the houses (of Gopńęs, arbours of trees in the VŠĻõndńĀvana forest) in the company of Jńęvas created in himself through his MńĀyńĀ Potency which, under his supervision and control, invests them with the life-spirit, the senses and mind or intellect.

12. The words describing his excellent attributes, exploits and incarnations which are capable of destroying all the sins of the world and of conferring auspicious blessings, resuscitate, contribute to the beauty and secure sanctity to the world. But words which do not describe them (howsoever ornamental and elegant they be) are like decorations of a dead body.

13. The Almighty Lord who brings about the felicity and blessings to the great gods who abide by the injunctions laid down by him[2], has descended into the clan of the SńĀtvatas. It is reported that the Supreme Ruler now stays at Vraja spreading all around his glory, which, due to its being auspicious to all beings, is sung by gods.

16. (Now he covets the touch of the Lord‚Äôs hand and hopes that the Lord would fulfill his longing.) 17. 18. Though deputed by KaŠĻÉsa, I am sure, he will entertain no hostile attitude to me as his enemy. For the Immortal[3] Lord is Omniscient and can see everything in the universe. 19. How strongly I yearn for it! 20. Would that when he would, with his mighty arras, embrace me, his best friend and well-wisher, his clansman who look upon him exclusively as my God, my soul (body) will be highly sanctified immediately and the fetters of karman lie shattered thenceforth.

21. Fie upon the life of that being who is not respected in such a way by the worshipful Lord.

22. No one is beloved of him or an intimate friend of him nor is any one disagreeable, hateful or worthy of indifferent treatment to him[4]. He however, attends upon his devotees, just as the celestial wish-yielding tree grants[5] the desire of a person when he is resorting to it.

23. And moreover, his elder brother BalarńĀma. the best in the YaŠłću-clan, will embrace me with a smile. Ňörńę Ňöuka said:

24. Oh King, while AkrŇęra, the son of Ňövaphalka was contemplating about Lord KŠĻõŠĻ£ŠĻáa, he arrived by his chariot at Gokula, when the sun was reaching the setting mountain (the western horizon)

26. 28. There, in the enclosure meant for milking cows, he saw KŠĻõŠĻ£ŠĻáa and BalarńĀma wearing yellow and blue garments respectively and with eyes beautiful like lotuses in the autumn.

30. The noble-souled pair of brothers ornamented the (ground in) Vraja with (the prints of) their feet marked with the (figures of) a banner, a Vajra, a goad and a lotus and their smiling eyes expressed compassion.

31. They had taken their bath and had their person besmeared with fragrant pigments (such as sandal-paste). They wore wreaths of flower and VanamńĀlńĀs and were immaculately attired. They expressed noble yet attractive playfulness.

32. They were the Foremost and the first ancient-most Persons, the cause of the world and the protectors thereof. They had descended on the world in their personal rays ( aŠĻÉŇõas) as BalarńĀma and KŠĻõŠĻ£ŠĻáa for the good of the world.

33. By their personal splendour they dispelled the darkness of the directions (all over the world). They appeared respectively like a mountain of emerald and a mount of silver both chased in gold[6], Oh King!

34. So overwhelmed with devout affection was that AkrŇęra that he quickly jumped down from the chariot, and fell prostrate like a stick near the feet of BalarńĀma and KŠĻõŠĻ£ŠĻáa.

35. With his eyes flooded with tears of joy at the sight of the glorious Lord and with hair standing all over his body, he was not able even to introduce himself to them as his throat was choked with love and emotion.

36. Being affectionate to those who have completely surrendered themselves to him, the Lord who understood him (as to who he was and the purpose of his arrival etc.), drew AkrŇęra near him with his hands, characterised with the mark of discus, and embraced him as he was pleased with him.

37. The noble-minded BalarńĀma embraced AkrŇęra who was bowing low before him. Holding his clasped palms by his own, he led him to his house along with his younger brother KŠĻõŠĻ£ŠĻáa.

38. After enquiring about his journey (whether he faced any troubles etc.) and offering him a high seat, BalarńĀma washed his feet with due formality and offered him articles of Madhuparka (such as honey, ghee, curds, water).

39. Having presented a cow to the guest and after massaging him as he was fatigued, the powerful Lord served to him with reverence, preparation of pure food with a variety of excellences.

40. When his meal was over, BalarńĀma, the expert in the highest dharma (viz. proper reception of guest) again delighted him by offering articles like fragrant betel-leaf, scenting his mouth, perfumes and sweet-smelling garland.

41. When the customary formalities were over, Nanda enquired: ‚ÄúOh DńĀŇõńĀrha (Descendant of DaŇõńĀrha)! How do you manage to get on with the regime of ruthless KaŠĻÉsa‚ÄĒyou who are now no better than sheep tended by a butcher.

43. Having thus been honoured by Nanda in sweet words, AkrŇęra recovered from the fatigue of the journey by such polite enquiries.
